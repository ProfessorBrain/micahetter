Cranial Chaos Gameplay: Illustration
This is an illustration of the game play for single players. For ease, we will use four players,
each with three of the cranial nerves represented in their hands, and four rounds of play to
cover an example for each nerve. This can also be used for teams, with one player
representing each team.
Players: Abigail, Benji, Carson, and Dominic
Each draw three cards and since Abigail was the last player to go on rotation at the hospital,
she will be the judge in round one.
Round One:
Abigail turns over the top card of the Axon Deck to read. The remaining three players for this
round will choose the best answer they have available in their hands and justify their choice
with an explanation. Once all players laid their choice face down, they may reveal and give
their justification to the judge starting with the player to the judge’s left.

Round Three:
Carson is the judge and reveals the prompt.
Singing along to
the radio in your
car.
− Dominic begins: “Perfect! I have CN VIII – Vestibulocochlear Nerve. This way I can
hear the radio and make sure I'm in sync with the music.”
− Abigail goes next: “Well I’m not a good singer and knowing my luck, I would take a sip
of water or something between songs and end up activating my gag reflex and
coughing. So, I am going to play CN IX – the Glossopharyngeal Nerve, since that is the
afferent part of that reflex.”
− Benji ends the round: “I love singing, but without your vocal cords moving, you can’t!
I’m going to use CN X – the Vagus Nerve, because the recurrent laryngeal branch of
vagus nerve innervates the intrinsic muscles of the vocal cords so I can sing in tune.”
Carson awards the round to Benji for his point about the recurrent nerve.

Round Four:
Dominic turns over the top card, but rather than a prompt, a bonus card is revealed,
“Synaptic Snag +1”. Dominic knows to turn over a second Axon card for a prompt.
The bonus card being turned over first indicates that for this round, the players must include
in their answers where the nerve they are discussing synapses, should they add the accurate
information and win the round, they will receive Two points this time.
Watching the Big
Summer
Blockbuster in a
theater.
− Abigail starts the last round: “I’m playing CN V – the Trigeminal Nerve, which does
many things but I'm going to focus on V3 – the Mandibular branch. This branch
innervates the muscles of mastication, and I can’t see a movie without some
popcorn! Oh, and because that’s motor, it should synapse on the target muscles.”
− Benji’s turn: “I also love popcorn, but I like stealing it out of my partner’s bucket when
they are watching movies. So, I’m going to use CN VI – Abducens Nerve, that way I
can use my lateral rectus muscle to peek at them beside me, so I know when to strike.
That’s also purely motor so it will synapse right on the lateral rectus muscle.”
− Carson will end the game: “I’m picking CN III – Oculomotor. Most of my eye
movement to follow the screen will be done with this one, and I have a bad habit of
falling asleep in theaters so I’m going to use the levator palpebrae superioris to keep
my eyelids open, and that will also synapse directly on the muscle.”
Dominic considered the more complex answers, deciding on Abigail’s because of creativity.
Abigail received Two points for the round, making her the WINNER of the game.

These are of course just examples of ways players could respond to a given prompt for the
different cranial nerves. Depending on the number of players and rounds, as well as the
individual creativity in their justifications, there are nearly limitless possibilities for how
students can respond. I find that this encourages students to recall less obvious or discuss
innervations of the cranial nerves and give the game plenty of “replay-ability” value.

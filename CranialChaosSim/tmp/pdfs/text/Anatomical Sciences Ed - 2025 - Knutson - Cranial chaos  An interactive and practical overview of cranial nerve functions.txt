A total of 202 first- year medical students participated in this study. 90.2% of the
184 respondents agreed or strongly agreed that the cranial nerve card game was
easy to use, 91.8% agreed or strongly agreed that the cranial nerve card game was
interactive, and 78.3% agreed or strongly agreed that the cranial nerve card game
facilitated a more practical understanding of cranial nerve functions. Cranial chaos
is an innovative, simple, and inexpensive teaching tool for reviewing cranial nerve
functions using real- life scenarios.

Themes
Competence
• “This activity helped me to parse out which nerves were involved in/responsible for which motor and sensory functions.”
• “The card game was a major component of me feeling much more confident in my knowledge of cranial nerves when lab was finished.”
• “I definitely came away from it with a stronger understanding and foundation of the nerves.”
• “It was a fun way to strengthen my knowledge of the cranial nerves and had a positive long- term impact on my retention of the knowledge.”
Autonomy
• “This game was fun and interactive, which was very helpful while trying to memorize a lot of information. I love it!”
• “This was a very fun activity and a chance to actively utilize our knowledge in a fun and engaging way.”
• “The cranial nerve game allowed for us to apply what we learned in a fun setting. I genuinely think it made it easier and less overwhelming for
me to remember the different functions of each cranial nerve.”
• “This was a fun way to interact with the material and made us think outside the box.”
Relatedness
• “The interactive nature of this activity allowed me to speak with other students in my structure group which helped me learn some of the
material.”
• “I enjoyed the collaborative aspect of this game with classmates.”
• “I didn't think much of the game at first, but it forced me to think of novel connections between the cranial nerves and our overall physiology.”
• “It helped me practice my knowledge by applying it to an activity that required thinking about the information in an unconventional way/
different from the straight- forward information regurgitation I am used to practicing.”
Drawbacks
Prior knowledge was necessary
• “This activity assumed everyone memorized all the cranial nerves which was not the case at all.”
• “I was lucky to have studied the cranial nerves before so I found the game to be very helpful in applying that information, but I think other
members on my team that had only done the prework the night before struggled to play the game since they had not really consolidated that
information yet.”
Limited amount of time
• “It was very quick, so I got a little bit out of it but if timing were better, we could've gotten through all of them.”
• “The idea was good, but the execution wasn't so great as we didn't really have enough time to go through an effective number of cards.”
Confusion about the rules
• “We jumped right into the card game without really understanding the rules/how to play. This made the first couple of rounds feel rushed and
didn't particularly enhance my understanding of the cranial nerves.”
• “The only bummer about the game was the fact that there was no direct ‘winner’ to the specific questions because some answers could be
‘argued’ to be a better fit for the question. More specific rules/guidelines would be helpful.”
Lack of variability of card options
• “I think having a wider variety of “action” cards would be beneficial.”
• “I think at times we repeatedly got the same cards or multiple of the same so mixing them up better might be helpful next time.”

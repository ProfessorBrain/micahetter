    About this quiz

    A simple question-based way to reflect on specialty fit.

    This is meant for first- and second-year medical students who want a rough starting point before clinical experience begins to narrow the field.

    How it works

    There are 102 A-or-B questions. Each answer adjusts the running ranking based on continuity, pace, procedures, uncertainty, population, and work setting, then uses that pattern to sketch nearby fellowship paths.

    What you see

    The specialty ranking updates as you answer more questions. Fellowship paths sharpen underneath those specialties as your answers become more specific.

    What it looks at

    What this quiz is measuring

    • Patient continuity versus episodic care
    • Procedures versus longitudinal management
    • Acute decision-making versus controlled pace
    • Breadth versus narrow domain focus
    • Diagnostic intensity, teamwork, and schedule shape

    Best Use

    Close results are common. They usually suggest a broader family of specialties rather than one clearly preferred field.

    Included Fields

    Internal Medicine, Family Medicine, Pediatrics, Medicine-Pediatrics, Emergency Medicine, General Surgery, Orthopedic Surgery, Anesthesiology, Psychiatry, Radiology, Neurology, Obstetrics & Gynecology, Dermatology, Ophthalmology, Otolaryngology, Urology, Physical Medicine & Rehabilitation, Pathology, and a broad set of 101 fellowship paths that sit within or alongside those homes.

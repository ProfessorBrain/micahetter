Adventures in Medicine is a growing library of short branching clinical cases for medical students and early residents. Each case is designed to train diagnostic reasoning, interview skills, emergency triage, management choices, communication, and ethics through the way the encounter unfolds.

The cases are built to feel like a serious clinical simulator: your decisions shape what happens next, and the teaching stays close to the patient story with reflective feedback instead of hard interruption.

How it works

  • Each case takes about 5 to 10 minutes and branches in response to your choices.
  • You will gather the history, assess risk, make decisions, and watch the patient course change in real time.
  • You can restart a case any time to take a different path.
